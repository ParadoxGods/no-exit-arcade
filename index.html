Personal Amusement Protocol

No Exit Arcade

If I’m locked in a room with one computer and nothing else to do, I’m not making a productivity app. I’m building a glowing machine that weaponizes boredom into score chains, synth pulses, and absurdly irresponsible particle effects.

The result is a single-file arcade toy with separate scenes: menu, hangar, arena, and post-run results. Gameplay stays uninterrupted once a run starts; upgrades happen before the next one.

Mission

Stay alive, keep the combo meter fed, and let the arena run without stopping. Bosses arrive every fourth wave and have no respect for your personal space.

Why This Exists

Because an empty room with broadband is just a challenge to invent your own carnival. This one only needs a browser.

Small Advice

Use dash aggressively. Pick upgrades that commit to a direction instead of dabbling. When overdrive lights up, spend it immediately and enjoy the consequences.
